01 — Brand Overview
Discovering the value in slowing down.
Kinfolk is a slow-living magazine founded in Portland, Oregon in 2011 and later relocated to Copenhagen. The name combines kin (family) and folk (friends). The brand defined the visual aesthetic of a generation — minimalist warmth, intentional living, and the beauty of small gatherings.

02 — Color Palette
Earth, linen, stone.
Kinfolk's palette is deliberately restrained. Muted earth tones create warmth without competing with photography. Color serves atmosphere, never decoration. The communicative role of colour — creating an “appealing grammar” — is one of the brand's defining characteristics.

03 — Typography
A compact, versatile palette.
Kinfolk's 2021 redesign introduced a custom type family by Schick Toikka: a serif and sans-serif pair with matching italics, sharing vertical dimensions so they mix harmoniously. The serif includes a Text style for body and a Display style with higher contrast for headlines.
Note: The custom Kinfolk typefaces are proprietary. This specimen uses Cormorant Garamond (display), EB Garamond (text), and Work Sans (sans) as open-source approximations that capture the spirit of the editorial approach.

What distinguished Kinfolk was never a single visual device but the quality of restraint — knowing when to let a photograph breathe, when to leave a page half empty, when to set a headline in 72 points and when to whisper it in 9. The design created an appealing grammar of color and composition that readers came to associate with a way of living, not merely a magazine.

05 — Photography Direction
Soft light. Quiet subjects.
Photography is the primary carrier of the brand. Every image follows the same principles: natural daylight, generous negative space, organic textures, and an embrace of imperfection. The wabi-sabi influence is deliberate — wrinkled linens, slightly irregular ceramics, wilting flowers are beautiful because they are real.
[ portrait ]
Profile in natural window light.
Linen shirt. Bare wall.
Eyes looking away from camera.
[ still life ]
Handmade ceramic on oak table.
Single dried stem. Soft shadow.
Shallow depth of field.
[ gathering ]
Hands reaching for bread.
Wooden table, mismatched plates.
Candlelight, golden hour.
[ space ]
Empty room. Stone floor.
Single chair near window.
Morning light on plaster wall.
[ landscape ]
Coastal scene. Overcast sky. Muted colors. A single figure in the distance. Generous negative space above.
Always
Soft, diffused natural daylight. Generous breathing room. Organic textures and materials. Candid moments over posed. Imperfection as beauty.
Never
Flash or harsh artificial light. Cluttered compositions. Glossy or synthetic surfaces. Stock photography. Over-saturated color.
06 — Design Principles
How Kinfolk designs.
01
Warm minimalism
Not cold or clinical. Minimalism with texture, warmth, and humanity. Restraint in service of feeling, not austerity.
02
Negative space is content
Empty space is a deliberate compositional tool. It creates calm, focus, and room for the reader's imagination. Never fill space for the sake of filling it.
03
Photography leads
Images carry the emotional weight. Typography and layout serve the photography, never compete with it. When in doubt, let the image breathe.
04
Classic meets contemporary
Timeless quality balanced with modern sensibility. Serif elegance with sans-serif utility. Traditions respected, never slavishly followed.
05
Grammar of color
Color is communicative, not decorative. The muted palette creates atmosphere. Saturated color is an event, not a default.
06
Evolution over revolution
The brand evolves gradually. Each redesign refines rather than reinvents. Consistency over years is what builds recognition.

08 — Voice & Tone
Quiet, considered, warm.

The voice is

Unhurried. Literate. Attentive to detail without being precious. It reads like a conversation with someone who thinks before they speak and chooses their words carefully — not to impress, but because precision is a form of respect.

The voice is not

Urgent. Clever. Performative. Selling. It never chases trends, never shouts, never uses exclamation marks as a substitute for genuine enthusiasm. It does not need to convince you of anything — it assumes you already share its values.

Kinfolk tone in action
“This issue is an exploration of what it means to entertain with purpose — to set a table not for show but for the conversation that happens around it. The best gatherings are rarely the most elaborate. They are the ones where someone thought about who should sit next to whom.”
09 — Brand Evolution
2011 – 2015
Rustic warmth
DIY sensibility. Handmade feel. Blurry watercolors. Pale, restrained color. Adobe Caslon. Garamond. Japanese-influenced whitespace. Portland roots.
2015 – 2021
Refined editorial
Charlotte Heal redesign. Garamond + Avenir + Fortescue. Left-aligned headers. Full-bleed images. Illustrators introduced. Copenhagen relocation. Gallery-like polish.
2021 – Present
Custom maturity
Alex Hunting Studio. Custom Schick Toikka type family. New grid, paper stocks, logo. Serif + sans sharing proportions. Architectural precision. The aesthetic codified.
10 — Relevance to WayFolk
What translates. What doesn't.

Carries over

The muted warmth of the palette. The restraint — knowing what not to include. Serif elegance for emotional moments paired with sans-serif for utility. Typography as the primary design tool. The “grammar of color” approach where tones create atmosphere. The principle that quality of attention matters more than volume of information.

Needs adapting

Kinfolk is built around stillness; WayFolk is about movement and connection. Kinfolk's photography centers objects and spaces; WayFolk centers people. Kinfolk's pace is slow and contemplative; WayFolk needs to work at travel speed, on mobile, between flights. The refinement needs to survive a 15px notification on a phone screen, not just a printed spread.
