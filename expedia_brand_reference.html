Brand Reference — Reconstructed

Expedia

The 2021+ identity: yellow chiclet, blue arrow, Travel Sans, and the "Made to Travel" visual language. A reference reconstruction for WayFolk.

01 — Logo & Mark

The Yellow Chiclet

The 2021 rebrand replaced the classic blue circle + yellow airplane with a yellow square ("chiclet") containing a dark blue arrow pointing northeast. The arrow represents direction and forward movement across all travel modes — not just flights.

Logo Rules

Three Versions

Full logo (chiclet + wordmark) above 48px height. Icon (chiclet only) below 48px. Wordmark alone for narrow spaces.

Clear Space

Safe area equals the x-height of "expedia". Minimum width: 54px web, 19mm print. Never crop tighter than provided assets.

The Arrow

Points northeast — upward and to the right. Represents direction, movement, exploration. Not a plane. Not a compass. Pure forward momentum.

02 — Color System

Navy + Yellow

A disciplined two-color system. Deep navy for trust and sophistication. Vibrant yellow for warmth and optimism. The rule: limit to three colors or fewer per composition. Every additional color must earn its place.

Usage Rules

The Rule

Three colors or fewer per composition. Navy and yellow carry 90% of the brand. White and off-white for surfaces. Grey for text hierarchy.

Yellow as Signal

Yellow is the attention color — CTAs, highlights, the chiclet, pricing emphasis, status badges. It's warm but not decorative. Every yellow element should point the user forward.

Navy as Foundation

Navy is the trust color — headers, primary text, the arrow, navigation. Deep enough to feel premium, warm enough to avoid corporate coldness.

03 — Typography

Travel Sans & System Fonts

The 2021+ system uses Travel Sans — a custom typeface by Commission Studio with ABC Dinamo, designed specifically for Expedia and Hotels.com. It's inspired by transit signage (Johnston, airport wayfinding) — lowercase-focused, geometric, with travel infrastructure DNA.

Travel Sans — Display & Headings
It matters who you travel with

Custom typeface by Commission Studio + ABC Dinamo. Heavy weight for brand statements, regular for product headings. Lowercase-dominant. Inspired by train station and airport signage — a typographic homage to travel infrastructure.

Body & UI — Clean Sans
Your trip to Lisbon

Inter or system sans-serif for body text, descriptions, metadata, form labels. Clean and functional. Weight hierarchy: 400 for body, 500 for emphasis, 600 for UI labels. Pairs with Travel Sans without competing.

Expressive vs. Functional
Expressive — Campaign
Made to travel

Heavy weight, large scale, brand yellow for emphasis. Used in campaign hero moments, social media, brand films. The type is the visual — no decoration needed.

Functional — Product
Flights to Tallinn

Semi-bold headings, regular body. Clean hierarchy through weight, not drama. Information-dense layouts stay scannable because the type system is disciplined.

04 — Photography

"Made to Travel"

The 2022 campaign (Anomaly London, narrated by Ewan McGregor) shifted Expedia's imagery from glossy destination marketing to authentic, emotional travel moments. The things you remember — not the things on postcards.

Style

Vibrant, saturated, warm. Cinematography-quality production values but candid in feel. Natural light preferred. Eye-level perspective — never aerial or drone-like. Slight warmth in the color grade.

Subjects

Real travel moments: hands reaching for food, wet cobblestones at night, a hotel window at dawn, strangers at a bar, unpacking a suitcase. The rituals, not the monuments.

The Yellow Thread

The brand yellow appears "accidentally" within scenes — a lemon on a table, a taxi door, morning light on a wall, a neon sign. It connects disparate moments into one visual language without branding the image.

Do / Don't

Do

Candid moments, eye-level shots, warm natural light, slight grain, real textures (cobblestones, linens, weathered surfaces), people in context not posing, authentic rooms at authentic angles, 2880px+ on longest side.

Don't

Fisheye or telephoto distortion, aerial/drone shots, glossy stock photography, exaggerated room sizing, famous landmarks as hero images, overly posed groups, cold blue-white color grading, tourist clichés.

Visual Motifs in Campaign

Lemons

The "Lemons" spot uses lemons as a recurring visual thread connecting Italy, Mexico, and Southeast Asia. One subject, many places — visual storytelling over destination marketing.

Wayfinding

Arrows, coordinates, directional signage woven into layouts. The design language of airports and train stations repurposed as brand texture.

Recurring Color

Brand yellow appears organically in scenes — sunlight, taxis, fruit, textiles. Connects images without a logo. The yellow is a visual signature, not a stamp.

05 — Graphic Language

Arrow, Chiclet, Wayfinding

Expedia's graphic language is built from three elements: the arrow (direction), the chiclet (the brand container), and wayfinding patterns borrowed from travel infrastructure — signage, coordinates, routes.

The Arrow

The northeast-pointing arrow is both logo and motif. It appears at all scales — from the chiclet icon to full-bleed campaign graphics. It always points forward and up. Pure momentum, no ambiguity.

northeast forward momentum all travel modes not a plane

The Chiclet

The yellow square with rounded corners is the brand's most recognizable container. It holds the arrow, but also works as a framing device — photography, callouts, and UI elements can adopt its proportions and radius.

rounded square 16px radius brand container scalable

Wayfinding Patterns

Directional arrows, GPS coordinates, route lines, departure/arrival typography. Borrowed from airports, train stations, and transit systems. These patterns appear as texture in layouts — backgrounds, section dividers, decorative accents.

coordinates route lines transit signage directional

Geometric Contrast

Rounded shapes (chiclet, buttons, cards) balanced with sharp directional elements (the arrow, coordinate text). Softness for accessibility and approachability, sharpness for confidence and clarity. The tension is deliberate.

rounded + sharp soft containers crisp arrows deliberate tension

Design Tokens (Reconstructed)

Radius

Small: 6px (tags, badges). Medium: 12px (cards, inputs). Large: 16px (hero cards, chiclet). Pill: 999px (buttons, chips, search bar).

Spacing

Base unit: 4px. Card padding: 16–24px. Card gap: 12–16px. Section padding: 48–64px. Touch target minimum: 44px.

Elevation

Level 0: flat (default cards). Level 1: subtle shadow (hover states, dropdowns). Level 2: pronounced shadow (modals, popovers). Shadows are warm-tinted, not pure black.

07 — Tone of Voice

"It Matters Who You Travel With"

Expedia positions itself as a knowledgeable travel companion — friendly, encouraging, and slightly playful. Never corporate. The voice is peer-to-peer: "we're all travelers here."

Playful, Not Frivolous

Humor is welcome but never at the expense of clarity. The brand busts out personality in headlines and campaigns, then steps back to be clean and functional in the product. Personality has a place — and it's not the checkout flow.

Peer-to-Peer

Never talks down. Gives readers credit for intelligence. Doesn't over-explain. Sounds like a well-traveled friend sharing a recommendation, not a salesperson qualifying a lead.

Forward-Looking

"We are the world's travel platform. We bring the world within reach." Optimistic, ambitious, but grounded in enabling — not showing off. The traveler is the hero, Expedia is the tool.

Emotionally Honest

The "Made to Travel" campaign acknowledged that travel is emotional — not just transactional. The brand doesn't shy away from why travel matters (connection, growth, wonder), but it never gets saccharine.

Contextually Flexible

Tone flexes by context: warm and inspiring in marketing, clear and helpful in the product, urgent and direct in transactional moments. Not one voice — one character at different volumes.

Inclusive

"For everyone" isn't a tagline, it's a design constraint. Language and imagery default to diversity. Accessibility isn't an add-on — it shapes the color system, type scale, and interaction patterns.

08 — Relevance to WayFolk

What WayFolk Can Learn

Expedia is a commercial travel platform at scale. WayFolk is an intimate social travel graph. Different products, but several Expedia decisions are instructive.

Photography as Brand

Expedia's "yellow thread" — brand color appearing organically in photography — is a powerful technique. WayFolk's earth tones could work similarly: terracotta doorways, sage-colored shutters, sky-colored harbour light. The palette should feel discovered in the world, not applied to it.

Transit Typography

Travel Sans was inspired by Johnston and airport signage. WayFolk's Work Sans serves a similar role — a geometric sans with wayfinding clarity. The idea of typography that "smells like airports and train stations" is powerful for travel brands.

Disciplined Color

Expedia's three-color-max rule is instructive. WayFolk has 23 named colors, but the monochrome-first principle achieves the same discipline: warm neutrals carry 90%, accent colors earn their moments.

Two Registers

Expedia has the same brand/product split: "Made to Travel" campaigns are cinematic and emotional; the booking flow is clean, functional, information-dense. WayFolk's two-register principle (warm brand layer vs. precise product layer) mirrors this exactly.

Chiclet as Container

The rounded yellow square is both logo and design element. WayFolk could develop a signature shape — perhaps derived from the globe or a connection arc — that works at all scales from favicon to hero graphic.

The Differentiation

Expedia is commercial, transactional, at-scale. WayFolk is intimate, social, community-driven. Where Expedia says "book now," WayFolk says "say hello." Where Expedia shows hotels, WayFolk shows people. Same DNA (authentic travel moments), completely different product personality.
