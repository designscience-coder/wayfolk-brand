Reconstructed Brand Reference

ElevenLabs

The voice of AI. A brand system built around sound made visible — Chladni patterns, voice signatures, and a monochrome-first palette with nine expressive hues. Rebranded by Basement Studio × AREA 17.

01 — Logo

The Wordmark & Symbol

The 11 icon abstracts the number eleven while referencing the pause icon from audio players — a dual symbolism connecting the company name to its core technology. The wordmark uses a custom typeface with subtly condensed, squarish letterforms that echo the icon's verticality.

Clear Space

Minimum clearance equal to the logo's full height on all sides. All communications must maintain this area unobstructed.

Color Modes

Black on light backgrounds (primary), white on dark/photography. No other color application. Never recreate with different fonts.

Restrictions

No stacking, no shorthand versions, no effects or strokes, no rotation, no stretching. The logotype is fixed and inviolable.

02 — The 11 Symbol

Two Lines, Double Meaning

The standalone symbol works independently of the wordmark — on app icons, favicons, and as a brand shorthand. Its placement follows strict centering rules with generous surrounding space.

Eleven ‖ Pause

The numeral 11 and the audio pause icon occupy the same visual form. This conceptual density makes it one of the more clever tech logos — instantly recognizable, deeply meaningful, and irreducibly simple.

App Icons

Circle or square holding shapes. Icon surrounded by space equal to 3× the icon height for proper breathing room.

Placement

Always centered both vertically and horizontally within its container. Avoid placing near edges or in corners.

Model Naming

Uses the 11 symbol + model name in the Eleven font + version number. Example: ‖ Eleven English V2.

05 — Graphic Language

Sound Made Visible

Three signature graphic elements connect the visual identity to audio technology: Chladni patterns (physics of sound), Sound Flow (waveform visualization), and Voice Signatures (visual fingerprints of individual voices). Each has static, resting, and reactive states.

Chladni Patterns

Geometric shapes created by sound vibrating a surface. A physical phenomenon turned brand metaphor — how sound creates structure and beauty. Generated via a custom tool that transforms math into remixable brand graphics.

Sound Flow

A layered waveform visualization using 7 gradient stops across the color palette. Described as "warm chrome" — engineering precision blended with human warmth. Three states: static, resting (calm animation), reactive (responds to audio input).

Voice Signatures

Visual fingerprints of individual voices. Pitch maps to lightness, expressivity to saturation, timbre to texture (smooth/blurred vs rough/sharp). Hue is randomly assigned. Each voice gets a unique visual identity.

07 — Tone of Voice

Efficient, Simple, Clear, Fresh

ElevenLabs writes with the precision of engineering and the directness of conversation. No vague advertising language, no jargon, no filler. Active voice, verifiable claims, and respect for the reader's time.

Efficiency

Brief, well-engineered language. Every word earns its place. Respect the reader's time — if something can be said in fewer words, it should be.

Simplicity

Everyday speech over formal or legal language. Active voice over passive. If your grandmother wouldn't understand it, rewrite it.

Clarity

Verifiable claims only. No "groundbreaking," no "paradigm shift," no vague superlatives. State what the technology does, not what adjectives you wish applied to it.

Freshness

Engaging titles, moving narrative, minimal scene-setting. Get to the point, make it interesting, and don't pad the introduction.

Avoid

"Our groundbreaking, state-of-the-art voice synthesis platform leverages cutting-edge neural networks to deliver a paradigm-shifting audio experience."

Prefer

"Turn text into natural-sounding speech in 29 languages. Clone a voice in seconds."

Brand Name

Always "ElevenLabs" — capital E, capital L, no space, no variations. Never "Eleven Labs," "elevenlabs," or "ELEVENLABS."

Text Formatting

Sentence case everywhere. Left-aligned by default. Hanging punctuation for pullquotes. Enable "case-sensitive forms" when using uppercase.

Watermarks

Logo + uppercase product reference, bottom-right corner. Applied to free-tier content for brand awareness and safety signaling.

09 — Motion & Animation

Four Motion Systems

Motion in the ElevenLabs brand is never decorative — it's tied to audio behavior. Captioning syncs text to speech, Chime pairs voice signatures with typography, Echo layers sound flow with text, and the logo animation has two placement variants.

Captioning

By-word (expressive social), by-sentence (voice-overs), multilingual (language showcase). Text appearance synchronized with generated speech.

Chime

Combines Voice Signatures with kinetic typography. Two titling modes: linear left-to-right reveal and random appearance. Synced to ElevenLabs speech output.

Echo

Layers Sound Flow with typography in monochrome or the full 9-color palette. Expresses depth and emotional range of the voice technology.

11 — Relevance to WayFolk

What to Take Away

ElevenLabs is a very different product (B2B/prosumer AI tool vs. social travel graph), but several structural decisions in their brand system are directly applicable to WayFolk's visual identity build.

Adopt

Monochrome-first, color-as-expression

ElevenLabs' primary identity lives in black and white. Color is layered on top for emphasis, delight, and differentiation — never as the base. WayFolk could use a similar approach: a warm neutral foundation with chromatic color reserved for meaningful moments (globe interactions, folk connections, travel signals).

Adopt

Systematic color architecture

9 hues × 11 tints with semantic rules (text = 800, bg = 200, icon = 600) is a remarkably scalable system. WayFolk doesn't need 99 colors, but adopting the same structural thinking — a small set of hues with predictable tint ladders and semantic assignments — would make the brand system usable across dark mode, light mode, and the globe interface.

Adapt

Signature graphic rooted in product

Chladni patterns and Voice Signatures aren't decorative — they're the physics of what ElevenLabs does, made visual. WayFolk's equivalent is the globe and the social graph. The graphic language should emerge from the product's core mechanic (connections across geography) rather than from generic illustration.

Study

Custom typeface with product DNA

Eleven's Semi Condensed cut echoes the vertical 11 icon. The single-story "a" makes it distinct. A custom typeface is likely premature for WayFolk's stage, but choosing a font whose character echoes the brand's visual motifs (warmth, openness, the circular globe) would create similar coherence.

Adapt

Functional vs. expressive modes

ElevenLabs has two typographic modes: uppercase/wide-spaced for marketing impact, standard-case for product clarity. WayFolk will need a similar distinction — the waitlist page and social content need to feel different from the in-app travel graph, while remaining recognizably the same brand.

Adopt

AA compliance as identity, not afterthought

Building accessibility into the color system's foundations (rather than auditing later) means every future design decision is automatically compliant. With WayFolk's globe interface in both light and dark mode, this approach would prevent a mountain of rework.

Study

The icon that means two things

The 11 symbol is both the brand name and a universal audio control. That double-reading makes it instantly memorable. WayFolk's wordmark — particularly how "Folk" appears — could aim for a similar conceptual density, where the visual form carries meaning beyond the letters themselves.

Differ

Cold precision → warm human energy

ElevenLabs' tone is engineering-first: efficient, no-nonsense, enterprise-credible. WayFolk's approved brand voice is warmer, more conversational, and people-centered. The visual system should reflect this — softer radii, warmer neutrals (Kinfolk's territory), and more organic graphic elements than ElevenLabs' geometric precision.
